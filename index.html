About Me

I was born in Cotabato City, one of the largest cities located west of the Mindanao region in the Philippines, and moved here to the US when I was only 7. I grew up in Bronx, New York with my parents, older brother, and younger sister, and am currently residing in Stony Brook, New York as an undergraduate student in my second-year at Stony Brook University.

I decided to pursue a path in Computer Science, particularly Game Development, because I was always inspired by the amount of entertainment that games have given me since I was a kid, and I hope to do the same for future generations as a future game developer myself. Aside from just being able to graduate and get a stable job, computer science is also one of the main reasons why I'm still motivated to learn since it is not an easy subject to grasp and master, which is what I hope to do in the 4 years that I am here.

Besides academics, I am also part of PUSO Modern, an urban/hip-hop dance team affiliated with the mother organization PUSO, or Philippine United Student Organization, at Stony Brook University that competes in several competitions along the east coast circuit and showcases in various campus-wide events. I also enjoy creating videos and coding simple, interactive games in my spare time when I am not busy with school.
